Diploma recognition (Nuffic / IDW)

  • • What: official evaluation of a foreign diploma's Dutch level. Apply via idw.nl; Nuffic evaluates.
  • • Cost: ~€150–200 (+ sworn translation €100–300 if documents aren't in EN/NL).
  • • Time: ~10 working weeks, up to ~4 months — start early.
  • • Need: diploma + transcript of records (certified / translated).
  • • Not strictly mandatory for HSM, but strengthens positioning and is often requested.

Payroll / Employer of Record (EoR)

  • • How: a recognised-sponsor agency formally employs Ritah and runs the IND sponsorship while she works for a smaller eco firm that isn't itself a sponsor.
  • • Agencies: Brunel, All About Expats, and others — verify recognised-sponsor status before relying on them.
  • • Watch: agency margin/fee; the salary must still clear €5,942 gross/month.

Market, culture & target employers

  • • Targets: Arcadis, Royal HaskoningDHV, Sweco, Tauw — large international consultancies, commonly IND recognised sponsors (verify in the register).
  • • Application norms: concise 2-page CV, no photo / age / marital status, short motivation letter; many roles are English-first.
  • • Culture: direct, punctual communication; networking on LinkedIn matters. Recruiters for internationals e.g. Undutchables.
  • • Pay: 8% holiday allowance on top of salary; ask employers about the 30% ruling (skilled-migrant tax benefit — terms change, verify current rate).

Working now — bridge income & job search

Short-term income to fund the relocation runway in the Nijmegen / Arnhem region. This is a bridge only — it does not count toward the Highly Skilled Migrant route, so keep the main effort on the €5,942 sponsor search.

Work-permit rules for a non-EU student (read first)

  • • Hours: either max 16 h/week year-round OR full-time in June–August only — never both in the same period.
  • • Permit (TWV): the employer applies for a free work permit via UWV. Budget ~5 weeks, not days — so a June start realistically means working from late July.
  • • No freelance / ZZP: Temper, YoungOnes, Uber etc. require self-employment, which non-EU students may not do without IND approval. Only a normal employment contract (agency or direct) is safe.
  • • Why it matters extra: breaching permit conditions can later jeopardise the HSM application. Also keep valid health insurance + BSN.
  • • Verify first: exact rights depend on her specific residence permit — confirm on the document / with the exchange coordinator before signing anything.

Best channels (ranked for a 37-year-old MSc professional)

  • • 1 · Radboud University — paid assistant in her field. A research / student-assistant role in an environmental / sustainability group pays better than horeca, builds Dutch experience, generates the professor references already on the plan, and the university handles TWV routinely. The only "side job" that also advances the HSM goal.
  • • 2 · Multilingual professional agencies. Undutchables, Adams Multilingual Recruitment, Abroad Experience, Octagon Professionals, Together Abroad — English-first office / project roles, better paid than student gigs.
  • • 3 · Expat job boards. English-language vacancies across NL; filter for Nijmegen / Arnhem.
  • • 4 · Student agencies (pure bridge / plan B). ASA Talent and YoungCapital in Nijmegen are fast for summer cash, but treat as a fallback, not a direction.

Tactics

  • • Move fast on the permit: with the ~5-week TWV lead time, target employers who will wait for a steady July–August worker (warehouses, universities) over walk-in shifts.
  • • One-page bridge CV: contact details + languages + availability for in-person / walk-in applications, separate from the senior relocation CV.
  • • In-person beats online here: a visit to the Radboud careers office or an agency branch can shave weeks off the process.
  • • Keep it ring-fenced: bridge work funds the runway; it must not consume the time reserved for the sponsor / HSM search.
